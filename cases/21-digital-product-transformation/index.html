Case 21 · Digital Product Transformation

From Operational Excellence to Digital Product Strategy

An executive business case structured around diagnosis, transformation strategy, implementation, governance, and value realization.

1. Executive Summary

After leading operational excellence initiatives in multinational manufacturing environments, I partnered with a software developer to design and launch a mobile gaming application. The project demonstrated how Continuous Improvement principles can be successfully applied to Digital Product Management.

Status: Product launched — Early Market Validation

2. Business Challenge

  • Transform an idea into a viable digital product
  • Prioritize an MVP
  • Balance user value with technical feasibility
  • Create a product roadmap for continuous evolution

3. Diagnostic Approach

The project was structured around understanding the user problem before defining the solution. My contribution included:

  • Co-defining the product vision
  • Participating in feature prioritization
  • Applying Continuous Improvement thinking to product decisions
  • Working alongside the software developer during product creation
  • Supporting roadmap definition after launch

4. Key Hypotheses

  1. Continuous Improvement principles used in industrial operations can be effectively transferred to digital product development.
  2. An MVP-first approach reduces time-to-market while preserving user value.
  3. Iterative feedback loops (PDCA / Build-Measure-Learn) enable continuous product evolution.
  4. Cross-functional partnership between operational and technical expertise creates better product outcomes.

5. Recommended Transformation

  • Understand the user problem before designing the solution
  • Define a clear MVP scope
  • Prioritize features by user value
  • Launch quickly to collect real feedback
  • Continuously improve the product based on data

6. Transformation Roadmap

Phase 1 — Discovery and Definition | 0–30 Days

  • Define product vision and user problem
  • Conduct user research
  • Prioritize MVP features
  • Establish product roadmap

Phase 2 — Build and Launch | 31–90 Days

  • Develop MVP
  • Parallel testing and refinement
  • Launch application
  • Collect early market feedback

Phase 3 — Scale and Iterate | 3–12 Months

  • Analyze user data and behavior
  • Prioritize next features by value
  • Continuously improve product experience
  • Expand user base

7. Governance Model

The project was governed through direct partnership between operational excellence and software development expertise, using lean decision-making and regular alignment sessions.

Core Roles

  • Product Vision Lead: owns product strategy and user value
  • Software Developer: owns technical architecture and delivery
  • UX Researcher: owns user insights and validation
  • Continuous Improvement Lead: applies CI discipline to product decisions

8. KPI Framework

  • User adoption and retention rate
  • Feature usage frequency
  • Time from idea to deployment
  • User satisfaction score
  • Build-measure-learn cycle speed

KPIs should include clear definitions, owners, data sources, targets, thresholds, and required actions when performance moves outside tolerance.

9. Risks and Mitigations

Risk Mitigation
Solution without problem validation Start with user research before any build
Feature creep beyond MVP Strict MVP scope with prioritization framework
Technical feasibility delays Close partnership with developer from day one
Low early adoption Collect feedback and iterate rapidly
CI methods not adapted to digital Map CI tools to product management equivalents

10. Business Impacts and Results

The product is expected to generate user value, market validation, and revenue while demonstrating the portability of Business Transformation and Operational Excellence capabilities into digital product environments. Final results to be validated through market performance.

11. Executive Takeaway

Whether the product is physical or digital, sustainable value comes from understanding the problem before designing the solution. Business Transformation and Operational Excellence capabilities translate naturally into Digital Product Strategy.
