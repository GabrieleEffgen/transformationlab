Case 23 · Operational Excellence

Global Bottleneck Prioritization Framework

An executive business case structured around diagnosis, transformation strategy, implementation, governance, and value realization.

1. Executive Summary

I designed a structured framework to identify, prioritize and eliminate production bottlenecks across a continuous manufacturing operation. The methodology combined process mapping, operational data, multidisciplinary execution and digital monitoring. After its success, the framework was shared with manufacturing sites across Europe and the United States as a best practice.

The framework established a repeatable decision-making model that linked operational data, governance and execution discipline, ensuring teams consistently focused on the constraints with the highest impact on business performance.

2. Business Challenge

Although operational data existed, the organization lacked a standardized method for determining which constraints most affected nominal production capacity and where improvement efforts should be concentrated. Teams worked on multiple improvement initiatives simultaneously, but without a clear prioritization mechanism, resources were spread thin and high-impact bottlenecks were not addressed first.

3. Diagnostic Approach

  • Identify the true operational constraints across a complex continuous manufacturing process
  • Prioritize improvement initiatives based on business impact rather than urgency
  • Coordinate multidisciplinary teams around a single prioritized agenda
  • Integrate bottleneck management into the existing Digital Factory platform
  • Create a scalable methodology that could be replicated across multiple manufacturing sites globally

4. Key Hypotheses

The strategy was built on five pillars:

Process Mapping — Complete end-to-end mapping of the manufacturing flow to understand the current-state value stream and identify all potential constraint points.

Critical Point Identification — Systematic analysis to locate the specific constraints reducing nominal production capacity, using operational data and direct observation.

Business Prioritization — Each identified bottleneck was scored by business impact, creating a clear ranking that directed resources to the highest-value constraints first.

Action Roadmap — Initiatives were separated into short-, medium- and long-term horizons, allowing quick wins to build momentum while structural solutions were developed.

Digital Integration — The framework was embedded into the End-to-End Digital Factory platform, enabling real-time tracking of bottleneck status, action execution and impact measurement.

5. Recommended Transformation

Bottleneck Prioritization Framework Structure:

Stage Purpose
Process Mapping Understand the end-to-end manufacturing flow
Critical Point Identification Locate constraints reducing nominal capacity
Business Prioritization Select highest-value bottlenecks
Action Roadmap Separate short-, medium- and long-term initiatives
Scrum Governance Weekly follow-up and accountability
Digital Monitoring Track execution and performance in real time

Governance Model:

  • Weekly multidisciplinary bottleneck resolution meetings using Scrum principles
  • Real-time dashboard tracking bottleneck status, ownership and impact
  • Escalation protocol for blocked or delayed initiatives
  • Monthly review with plant leadership

6. Transformation Roadmap

Phase 1 — Diagnosis and Framework Design (Weeks 1–3):

  • Complete process mapping of the manufacturing value stream
  • Critical point identification and bottleneck candidate listing
  • Business impact scoring and prioritization
  • Governance model definition and meeting structure design

Phase 2 — Pilot Execution (Weeks 4–8):

  • Launch of weekly Scrum-based bottleneck resolution meetings
  • Deployment of digital monitoring dashboard
  • Resolution of top-3 priority bottlenecks
  • Documentation of results and lessons learned

Phase 3 — Standardization and Scale (Weeks 9–12):

  • Framework documentation and standard operating procedure creation
  • Training materials development for site replication
  • Presentation to European and US manufacturing sites
  • Adoption as corporate best practice

7. Governance Model

  • 50% reduction in production scrap
  • 42% increase in productive capacity
  • Standardized bottleneck prioritization process established
  • Higher execution speed through multidisciplinary Scrum governance
  • Framework adopted as best practice and shared with sites across Europe and the United States
  • Real-time visibility into bottleneck status and resolution progress

8. KPI Framework

  • Without a structured framework, teams naturally gravitate toward the loudest problem rather than the most impactful one
  • Combining operational data with structured governance creates sustained execution discipline
  • Digital monitoring transforms bottleneck resolution from a reactive to a proactive process
  • A framework proven in one site gains rapid adoption when it is simple, documented and linked to measurable results
  • Multidisciplinary teams resolve bottlenecks faster than functional silos

9. Risks and Mitigations

The approach can be applied to any manufacturing or operational environment:

  1. Map the complete value stream to identify all potential constraint points
  2. Score bottlenecks by business impact to create an objective priority ranking
  3. Establish Scrum-based governance with weekly accountability and real-time tracking
  4. Integrate with existing digital platforms to ensure visibility and sustainability
  5. Document and scale the methodology across sites once proven

10. Business Impacts and Results

Improvement initiatives generate greater impact when organizations stop treating every problem as equally important. This framework established a repeatable decision-making model that linked operational data, governance and execution discipline, ensuring that teams consistently focused on the constraints with the highest impact on business performance. The global adoption of the framework across multiple manufacturing sites confirmed that structured prioritization, combined with digital monitoring and disciplined execution, is a scalable approach to operational excellence.
