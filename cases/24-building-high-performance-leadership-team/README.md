# Building a High-Performance Leadership Team

**Capability:** Leadership & Change  
**Portfolio:** The Transformation Lab  
**Author:** Gabriele Miranda


## 1. Executive Summary

The operation required more than technical improvements. Leadership routines were inconsistent, decisions were frequently escalated and managers had limited clarity regarding priorities, ownership and development expectations.

The transformation connected business goals, individual capability, coaching, performance reviews and daily execution into a single leadership system designed to strengthen managerial capability, increase accountability, improve decision-making and create a more autonomous, performance-oriented environment.


## 2. Business Challenge

The organization needed a people-centered transformation.

- **Reactive leadership** — Managers spent most of their time responding to urgent issues instead of anticipating risks and developing their teams.
- **Unclear accountability** — Priorities, decision rights and performance expectations were not consistently translated into leadership behaviors.
- **Limited autonomy** — Operational decisions were frequently escalated, reducing speed, ownership and confidence across the leadership structure.


## 3. Diagnostic Approach

A structured model connecting people, leadership and execution:

- **Assess** — Evaluate leadership maturity, business understanding, decision-making and people-management capability.
- **Align** — Translate strategic priorities into clear roles, expected behaviors and measurable leadership outcomes.
- **Develop** — Create individual development plans, coaching routines and practical learning through real business challenges.
- **Empower** — Strengthen decision rights, problem-solving ownership and confidence to act closer to the operation.
- **Sustain** — Embed leadership reviews, feedback loops and performance governance into the management system.


## 4. Key Hypotheses

- Leadership capability must be connected directly to business execution.
- Coaching becomes more effective when applied to real performance challenges.
- Autonomy grows when decision rights and expectations are explicit.
- Sustainable transformation requires routines that reinforce new behaviors.


## 5. Recommended Transformation

Development was integrated into daily management:

- Structured one-to-one coaching linked to real operational priorities.
- Clear decision boundaries and accountability for performance indicators.
- Regular leadership reviews combining business results and capability growth.
- Feedback routines focused on behavior, impact and next-step development.
- Succession and readiness discussions supported by observable evidence.


## 6. Transformation Roadmap

The leadership system was rolled out through a progressive management rhythm:

- **Daily** — Operational priorities, risk anticipation and decision ownership.
- **Weekly** — Coaching, performance review and removal of execution barriers.
- **Monthly** — Capability assessment, development progress and succession readiness.


## 7. Governance Model

The governance structure included:

- Executive Steering Committee for strategic alignment and resource decisions.
- Regular leadership reviews combining business results and people development.
- Feedback loops connecting frontline observations to management actions.
- Clear escalation paths for risks requiring senior intervention.


## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- Number of leaders progressed to expanded roles.
- Managerial autonomy and decision ownership index.
- Response time to operational risks and priorities.
- Accountability and leadership consistency scores.


## 9. Risks and Mitigations

- **Risk: Resistance from middle management** — Mitigation: Involve managers in co-designing the new leadership model.
- **Risk: Competing priorities** — Mitigation: Link leadership development directly to business results.
- **Risk: Lack of sustained focus** — Mitigation: Embed new routines into the existing management cadence.


## 10. Business Impacts and Results

- 2 leaders progressed to expanded roles.
- Higher managerial autonomy and decision ownership.
- Faster response to operational risks and priorities.
- Stronger accountability and leadership consistency.

High-performance teams are not created by pressure alone. They emerge when leaders have clarity, capability, trust and the authority to make decisions.


## 11. Executive Takeaway

Transformation becomes sustainable when behaviors and systems evolve together. Leadership capability must be connected directly to business execution. Autonomy grows when decision rights and expectations are explicit. Coaching becomes more effective when applied to real performance challenges. Sustainable transformation requires routines that reinforce new behaviors.
